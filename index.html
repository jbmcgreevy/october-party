FAQ

A few things that might be useful to know.

What does "Vintage Casual" mean?

Wear something vintage that you love — that's really all it means. We went full 1970s, but any era goes. Thrifted, inherited, or just something with a retro feel. No need to overthink it, and definitely no need to go out and buy anything new.

Will there be food?

Yes! We'll have hors d'oeuvres, catered BBQ, and drinks, with vegan and vegetarian options available.

Where should I park?

Blow Street is a small one-way street with very limited parking, so we recommend leaving the car a block or two away. Gresham Ave and Loughborough Ave are both great options with easy street parking.

Can I bring a plus one?

Please reach out to jaredbmcgreevy@gmail.com and let us know — we'd love to make sure we have a headcount!

What if I can't make it?

We'll miss you! Just shoot us an email at jaredbmcgreevy@gmail.com so we know not to expect you.

Do you have a registry?

We don't have a registry — your presence at the party is more than enough. We're just excited to celebrate with you!

Things to Do in St. Louis

Make a weekend of it — there's plenty to explore before or after the party.

The party is right near Tower Grove Park and the Missouri Botanical Garden — both are beautiful in October and worth a morning stroll.
Gateway Arch
Landmark · Free to visit

Ride to the top of the iconic 630-foot arch for sweeping views of the Mississippi River and St. Louis skyline.

gatewayarch.com →
Missouri Botanical Garden
Nature · Near the party

One of the top botanical gardens in the country. Stunning in fall foliage. 5 min from the party venue.

missouribotanicalgarden.org →
City Museum
Unique · All Ages

Part playground, part art installation, part madhouse — there's nothing else like it. Adults love it as much as kids.

citymuseum.org →
St. Louis Zoo
Family · Free Admission

One of the best free zoos in the country, located in Forest Park. A full day's worth of wildlife to explore.

stlzoo.org →
Forest Park
Outdoors · Free

Bigger than Central Park, and home to the Zoo, Art Museum, History Museum, and Science Center — all free.

forestparkforever.org →
Anheuser-Busch Brewery
Tour · Beer

Tour the iconic historic brewery and Clydesdale stables. Free beer samples at the end.

budweisertours.com →
The Delmar Loop
Neighborhood · Dining & Shopping

A lively stretch of independent restaurants, record shops, and live music venues. Great for a casual evening out.

visittheloop.com →
Soulard Neighborhood
Historic · Bars & Market

St. Louis's oldest neighborhood — home to the Saturday Farmers Market, blues bars, and French Creole architecture.

soulard.org →
Cherokee Street
Shopping · Near the party

St. Louis's go-to strip for vintage and antique shopping, with dozens of thrift stores, record shops, galleries, and bars. Right in our neighborhood and perfect for hunting down something to wear to the party.

Bevo Mill
Landmark · Near the party

A historic 1917 Dutch windmill built by Anheuser-Busch, right in our neighborhood. One of St. Louis's most distinctive landmarks and a great photo stop.

The Hill
Neighborhood · Italian

St. Louis's beloved Italian-American neighborhood, packed with old-school delis, bakeries, and restaurants. A great spot for lunch or a leisurely afternoon wander.

Francis Park
Outdoors · Near the party

A beautiful neighborhood park just a short walk from the venue, with open lawns, a fountain, and tree-lined paths. Perfect for a morning stroll before the party.

Mastodon State Historic Site
History · South of the city

The site of one of the earliest known human-mastodon encounters in North America. A fascinating museum and nature trails just a short drive from St. Louis.

mostateparks.com →
Cahokia Mounds
History · Illinois

A UNESCO World Heritage Site just across the river — the remains of a pre-Columbian Native American city that was once larger than London. Genuinely awe-inspiring.

cahokiamounds.org →
St. Louis Central Library
Architecture · Free

A stunning Beaux-Arts building that underwent a gorgeous renovation. Even if you're not a library person, it's worth stepping inside just to see the space.

slpl.org →
Ted Drewes Frozen Custard
Food · St. Louis Icon

A St. Louis institution since 1929, famous for their impossibly thick "concrete" frozen custard. Right on Chippewa, just around the corner from the party — and yes, we stopped here on our wedding day. You should too.

teddrewes.com →
